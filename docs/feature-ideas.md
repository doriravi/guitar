# Feature Ideas — Five Guitar-Skill Perspectives

Fresh feature ideas for the guitar reach-difficulty app, generated by role-playing **five
different guitar skill levels**. Each persona reacted to the app as it exists today and proposed
ranked ideas tied to real existing features/files. The unique thread throughout: the app's
**physics-based, per-finger reach engine + hand profile** is its standout differentiator — most
ideas lean into it.

> Generated 2026-06-16. Source engine references: `client/src/lib/fretboard.js`
> (`calcDifficulty`, `fingerGapUsage`), `client/src/lib/handProfile.js` (reach multiplier,
> personalized score, 5 reach levels), `chords.js`, `songs.js`, `progressions.js`, `scales.js`,
> `audio.js`, `pitchDetect.js`. Backend: Spring Boot + SQLite, JWT auth (USER/ADMIN roles),
> hand-profile persistence, Stripe (FREE/MONTHLY/YEARLY), Gemini hand-analysis.

---

## 1. Absolute Beginner

> *"This looks cool but also kind of terrifying. The hand camera + reach-difficulty idea makes me
> feel seen — the app cares whether MY hand can do a chord. But seven tabs and words like 'scales,'
> 'triplets,' 'YIN pitch detection' lose me instantly. I came to play *something* and I'm scared a
> chord will be too hard and I'll feel stupid and quit. Hold my hand and say 'start HERE.'"*

1. **"Start Here" guided first-session onboarding** — A short welcome flow after the hand scan:
   plain-words "your hand is best at these chords," then drops straight into playing one easy chord
   instead of leaving me to guess among 7 tabs.
   *Builds on:* AI camera analysis + 5-level reach rating (`handProfile.js`) → Chord finder filter.
2. **"Easiest chords for YOUR hand" beginner shortlist** — Auto-pick 4–5 lowest-difficulty chords
   for my hand profile, shown big and friendly — not the full 300-chord library or a 1-10 table.
   *Builds on:* difficulty table + personalized score (`fretboard.js` + `handProfile.js`), via the
   existing Chord finder difficulty filter.
3. **Plain-language labels + "what is this?" tooltips on every tab** — Beginner-friendly subtitles
   ("Triplets → 3-finger stretches," "Listen → check if you played it right") with a "?" explainer.
   *Builds on:* existing 7-tab UI + the 10-language i18n system (wording, not engine work).
4. **"Can my hand even do this?" confidence check** — Tapping any chord shows a friendly
   green/yellow/red "Easy for you / A stretch / Skip for now" with encouragement, not just a number.
   *Builds on:* personalized reach multiplier + score (`handProfile.js`) on `chords.js` voicings.
5. **"Did I get it right?" instant cheer using the mic** — After trying a chord, the app listens
   and celebrates "Yes! That's a C!" — turning chord recognition into a beginner feedback loop.
   *Builds on:* Listen tab mic chord recognition, tied to the beginner shortlist.
6. **Plain photo "finger map" per shortlist chord** — Labeled "pointer here, middle finger here"
   view instead of raw fretboard dots a beginner can't decode.
   *Builds on:* interactive fretboard (`fretboard.js`) + AI per-finger attribute data.

## 2. Casual Strummer / Hobbyist

> *"This is more than I need most of the time, but the difficulty filter is genuinely cool — it
> warns me a chord's a finger-pretzel BEFORE I waste 20 minutes. The thing is, I play SONGS: G to C
> to D over and over, and that's where I fall apart. The app scores how hard a chord is to HOLD,
> but says nothing about how hard it is to SWITCH. And barre chords make me want to quit — just give
> me the easy cheat version."*

1. **Chord-change difficulty score** — A 1-10 reach score for the *transition* between two chords
   (finger travel, simultaneous lift/replant, shared anchor fingers), shown in the progression view.
   *Builds on:* `fretboard.js` `calcDifficulty` + `chords.js`, surfaced in Progressions + Music editor.
2. **"Easier version of this chord" suggestions** — For any hard chord, suggest a lower-difficulty
   voicing or common cheat (Fmaj7 / mini-F instead of full barre F, Cadd9 that flows from G).
   *Builds on:* 300+ voicings in `chords.js` + Chord finder difficulty filter, ranked by `handProfile.js`.
3. **Beginner-friendly progression/song filter** — A toggle showing only progressions and famous
   songs whose chords are *all* below my reach level (no barre chords) — stuff I can play tonight.
   *Builds on:* `songs.js` + `progressions.js`, filtered by the 5-level reach rating.
4. **Slow-it-down chord change trainer** — Loops a single chord change at adjustable slow BPM with a
   visual "switch now" cue, gradually speeding up.
   *Builds on:* Music editor BPM + `audio.js` `playProgression` + the chord-change score (#1).
5. **Capo helper for easier shapes** — Suggest a capo position that lets me play a song with open
   chords instead of barre chords ("capo 3, play in G shapes instead of Bb").
   *Builds on:* fretboard note model + reach scoring (`fretboard.js`), `chords.js`, song keys (`songs.js`).

## 3. Intermediate Player

> *"I'd live in Chord finder and Progressions, and the personalized difficulty tables help me decide
> what's worth drilling for my short fingers. But the mic and tuner are one-shot toys — nothing
> connects them to a practice loop or remembers yesterday. What's missing is structure and memory: I
> can find a hard chord and see its score, but the app never tells me what to practice next, never
> times me, and never proves I'm getting better. The reach engine and the mic detection exist side by
> side but never talk."*

1. **Practice Sessions with progress tracking** — Logged practice mode recording chords/progressions
   drilled, BPM, reps, cleanliness over time, with a per-chord progress chart.
   *Builds on:* Music editor save flow + new backend practice/progress entities; rated vs `calcDifficulty`.
2. **Mic-verified chord drills (close the feedback loop)** — Drill mode shows a target chord, listens
   via mic, confirms whether I played it cleanly — pass/fail reps instead of just a number.
   *Builds on:* `pitchDetect.js` (YIN + FFT matching), `chords.js`, reach score from `fretboard.js`.
3. **Personalized practice plan / "what to drill next"** — A recommender that uses my hand profile +
   difficulty tables to queue a daily set (chords just above my reach level + struggled-with review).
   *Builds on:* `handProfile.js` (multiplier, 5 levels, personalized score) + Chords/Triplets tables.
4. **Chord-transition difficulty + transition drills** — Score the *change* between chords (finger
   travel, common fingers, fret jump) and generate drills for my worst transitions in a progression.
   *Builds on:* `fretboard.js` `fingerGapUsage` extended to chord pairs + Progressions tab.
5. **Metronome / tempo-ramp drills** — Built-in metronome that auto-ramps BPM as I succeed (+5 after
   three clean reps) and logs my top clean tempo per chord/transition.
   *Builds on:* Music editor BPM + `audio.js` for click, feeding the Practice Sessions log (#1).
6. **Reach-difficulty streaks & goals** — Lightweight daily goals/streaks on the practice log ("play
   three 7+ chords cleanly today") with badges as I unlock higher reach levels.
   *Builds on:* `handProfile.js` 5 reach levels + Practice Sessions backend + JWT per-user persistence.

## 4. Advanced Player

> *"The bones are better than most chord-finder apps — modeling reach in millimeters with diagonal
> span and a per-finger-pair gap function is the thing nobody else does. It could tell me *which two
> fingers* are fighting and why, personalized to my hand. But right now it's stuck at the diagnostic
> layer — it scores shapes, it doesn't *solve* them. I don't want a list of 300 voicings; I want the
> optimal fingering and the cheapest path between two chords. Lean all the way into the physics engine
> as an optimizer, not a calculator."*

1. **Fingering optimizer (which finger goes where)** — Given a voicing's fret/string set, search all
   legal finger assignments (1-4 + barre/thumb) and return the one minimizing total reach cost, with
   the runner-up for comparison.
   *Builds on:* `calcDifficulty` + `fingerGapUsage` as the cost function, `chords.js` input,
   `handProfile.js` to personalize the winning assignment.
2. **Chord-transition cost scoring (voice-leading aware)** — Score *movement* between chords: anchored
   common-tone fingers cost ~0, slides cost less than lift-and-reland, plus a hand-shift fret penalty.
   *Builds on:* the optimizer (#1) for chosen fingerings on both sides + `calcDifficulty` mm model +
   Music editor / `progressions.js`.
3. **Progression path optimizer (best voicing chain for a song)** — Shortest-path search (Viterbi/DP)
   over candidate voicings per chord, minimizing static difficulty + transition cost → easiest playable
   voicing chain for a tune.
   *Builds on:* #1 and #2 as node/edge costs + `progressions.js`/`songs.js` + Progressions UI + filters.
4. **Stretch drills from my weak finger-pairs** — Mine `fingerGapUsage` across the library to find the
   specific finger-pair stretches I personally fail, then generate a graded warm-up just past my ceiling.
   *Builds on:* `fingerGapUsage` + `personalDifficulty`/`abilityLabel` + Triplets/Chords tables + AI re-scan.
5. **Custom voicing editor with live per-finger feedback** — Place notes on the fretboard to build my
   own voicing, with real-time difficulty + a heat-map of the bottleneck finger-pair; save/name it.
   *Builds on:* Strings fretboard + Music editor save + live `calcDifficulty`/`fingerGapUsage` + optimizer (#1).
6. **"Reachable voicings for X" recommender** — Pick a target chord/quality; surface only voicings
   (inversions/subs) under my personal difficulty ceiling, ranked by ease, sorted by register.
   *Builds on:* Chord finder difficulty filter + `chords.js` + `personalDifficulty` + #1's fingering scores.

## 5. Professional / Teacher

> *"The reach-difficulty engine plus the hand profile made me sit up — I've got an 11-year-old with
> tiny hands and an adult with early arthritis, and right now I guess at what's playable by watching
> them struggle. Scoring a chord 1-10 *personalized to their hand* means I can finally assign shapes I
> KNOW they can reach. What's missing is everything that turns a solo practice toy into a studio tool:
> manage my roster, assign material, see who practiced, share a progression. Give me that layer on top
> of this engine and I'd pay monthly without blinking."*

1. **Teacher tier with student roster & seat management** — A TEACHER role/subscription to invite
   students, see each one's hand profile, and switch into their "view" to see chords scored for *their*
   hands.
   *Builds on:* existing USER/ADMIN roles + JWT + Stripe tiers — add TEACHER role, teacher↔student link,
   new Stripe price tier; hand-profile persistence already per-user.
2. **Assignments tied to Music Editor & Chord finder** — Push a song/progression/chord set/scale to a
   student as a dated assignment, with a difficulty cap based on their reach level.
   *Builds on:* Music editor (`songs.js`), Chord finder (`chords.js`), scales (`scales.js`),
   `handProfile.js` auto-cap; new assignments table.
3. **Practice tracking / progress dashboard** — Per-student timeline of what they worked on and whether
   assigned chords moved from "hard" toward "comfortable" as their profile improves.
   *Builds on:* `handProfile.js` 5-level reach rating as the metric + assignment completion (#2) + new
   analytics endpoints.
4. **Shareable, annotated progressions & lesson sheets** — Export/share a built progression (chords, BPM,
   lyric/chord annotations) as a link or printable sheet, already difficulty-scored for that student.
   *Builds on:* Progressions tab annotations + Music editor BPM; add share-link/PDF export.
5. **Adaptive chord substitutions for limited hands** — When a chord scores too high for a student's
   reach level, auto-suggest an easier voicing (partial barre, capo) that keeps the harmonic function.
   *Builds on:* `calcDifficulty` + `fingerGapUsage` + `chords.js` + `handProfile.js` (mostly client-side).
6. **Per-student notes & lesson history** — A notes field per student ("worked on F barre, still pinky
   pain — revisit in 2 weeks"), tied to their profile and assignment history.
   *Builds on:* teacher↔student link (#1) + small backend user/relationship addition.

## Cross-Cutting Themes & Top Recommendations

Several ideas recur across multiple skill levels — those overlaps are the highest-leverage bets.

### Theme A — Chord-transition scoring (asked for by 4 of 5 personas)
Casual, Intermediate, and Advanced all independently asked to score the *change* between chords, not
just the static shape; Beginner wants "did I switch right?" feedback. This is the **single most
requested missing capability** and it directly extends the existing engine.
- **Foundation:** extend `fingerGapUsage`/`calcDifficulty` in `fretboard.js` to operate on chord *pairs*
  (finger travel, common-tone anchors, hand-shift penalty).
- **Unlocks downstream:** casual "slow-it-down change trainer," intermediate transition drills, advanced
  transition-cost + progression-path optimizer.

### Theme B — The reach engine as an *optimizer*, not just a calculator
The app currently *diagnoses* difficulty. The biggest differentiator is to make it *prescribe*:
- **Fingering optimizer** (advanced #1) — the keystone; many other ideas depend on it.
- **Adaptive/easier substitutions** for hard chords — wanted by Casual (#2), Beginner (#4), Teacher (#5).
- **Progression path optimizer** (advanced #3) — turns the app from reference into arrangement tool.

### Theme C — Practice structure + memory + progress
Intermediate's whole list, plus Beginner's mic-cheer and Teacher's dashboard. Today the mic/tuner are
one-shot; nothing persists. Requires the **not-yet-built backend practice/progress entities** — a
significant but high-value investment that also enables streaks, teacher tracking, and progress proof.

### Theme D — Onboarding & plain language (lowest cost, immediate retention win)
Beginner's #1–#3 are mostly UI/copy on top of features that already exist (i18n is in place). Cheapest
ideas with outsized impact on whether new users stay past day one.

### Theme E — Teacher tier as a revenue path
Teacher #1–#3 layer a B2B2C product onto the existing roles + JWT + Stripe infrastructure. The
reach-difficulty + hand-profile angle is uniquely valuable for students with small hands or physical
limitations — a genuine "wow" demo and a defensible niche.

### Suggested sequencing
1. **Quick wins:** Beginner onboarding/plain-language (Theme D) + "easier version of this chord"
   substitutions (Theme B) — low cost, broad appeal, leverages existing engine.
2. **Engine deepening:** chord-transition scoring (Theme A) + fingering optimizer (Theme B) — the
   technical heart that unlocks the most downstream features and is the strongest differentiator.
3. **Platform investment:** backend practice/progress tracking (Theme C), then the teacher tier
   (Theme E) for monetization.
