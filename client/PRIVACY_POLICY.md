**Last updated:** 18 July 2026

FretFit ("the app") is a guitar practice tool. This policy explains what the app
accesses and how your data is handled. It is written for the current
**guest-mode** version of the app, which works without an account.

## What the app accesses

**Microphone.** The tuner, live-listen, and scale-practice features use your
device's microphone to detect the pitch of the notes you play. Audio is processed
**on your device in real time** to determine pitch. In guest mode, this audio is
**not recorded, stored, or transmitted** off your device.

**Camera.** The optional hand-measurement feature uses your camera to help gauge
finger reach. Camera frames are processed **on your device**. In guest mode, images
are **not stored or transmitted** off your device.

**Local storage.** Your settings and practice history (for example your hand
profile and saved practice results) are stored **locally on your device** so the
app remembers them between sessions. Uninstalling the app removes this local data.

## Data we collect
In guest mode, FretFit does **not** collect, store, or share personal information,
and does not use analytics or advertising trackers. The app functions fully
offline.

## Children's privacy
FretFit is a general-audience music tool and is not directed at children under 13.

## Permissions summary
- **Microphone** — pitch detection for tuner and practice (on-device).
- **Camera** — optional hand-measurement (on-device).
- **Internet** — used by the app shell; guest features work offline.
